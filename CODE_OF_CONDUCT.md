# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want CodeSpec to be a welcoming, respectful project for everyone who wants to improve spec-driven development for Power Apps Code Apps.

Project maintainers and contributors pledge to make participation in this community a harassment-free experience for people of every background, identity, and level of experience.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps create a positive environment include:

- using welcoming and inclusive language;
- respecting different viewpoints and experiences;
- giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully;
- focusing on what is best for the community and the project;
- showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or sustained disruption;
- insulting, derogatory, or personal attacks;
- sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and taking fair, appropriate corrective action when needed.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation for behavior they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all CodeSpec project spaces. It also applies when someone is representing CodeSpec in public spaces, such as using an official project channel, participating in project-related discussions, or acting as an appointed project representative.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers using the private contact method listed by the repository owner or maintainers. If no private contact is available, open a brief GitHub issue requesting a private reporting channel and do not include sensitive details in the issue.

Maintainers will review reports promptly and respond in a way they determine is necessary and appropriate. Maintainers are expected to respect the privacy and safety of the person reporting an incident.
